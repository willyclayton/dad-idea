## 1. Executive Summary

**FilterDrop** is a subscription-based AC filter delivery company with a data intelligence layer built on top of its customer base.

Consumers register their air conditioning units, receive correctly-sized replacement filters automatically on manufacturer-recommended schedules (30, 60, or 90 days), and opt into contributing anonymized unit data. That data is then licensed to HVAC repair companies, manufacturers, and property managers as actionable intelligence — creating a second, high-margin revenue stream that scales with subscriber count.

**The core insight**: every new subscriber is simultaneously a recurring revenue customer *and* a data point in a growing HVAC intelligence network. As the subscriber base grows, the data product becomes more valuable, which funds better customer acquisition, which grows the subscriber base further.

**Immediate target market**: US residential homeowners (approx. 93 million housing units with central air).

**Business model summary**:
- B2C subscriptions: $15–$45/month per household
- B2B commercial accounts: volume pricing + premium reporting
- Data licensing: HVAC contractors, manufacturers, insurers
- Referral commissions: aged-unit replacement leads

## 2. Problem & Opportunity

### The Consumer Problem

- **75% of homeowners** do not change AC filters on the manufacturer-recommended schedule
- Dirty filters reduce unit efficiency by 5–15%, increasing energy costs
- Neglected filters can void manufacturer warranties
- Most consumers do not know their filter size, MERV rating recommendation, or when they last changed it
- The average homeowner owns their AC unit for 12–15 years but replaces it closer to year 18 — long past optimal performance

### The HVAC Industry Problem

- HVAC repair and maintenance companies operate reactively — they respond to breakdowns, not predicted failures
- There is no centralized, real-time data on the age, brand mix, and condition distribution of installed AC units
- Manufacturers receive almost no field feedback on unit longevity, common failure modes by geography or climate, or real-world SEER performance
- Local HVAC contractors have no cost-effective way to identify households with aging units before a competitor does

### The Business Opportunity

| Segment | US Market Size |
|---|---|
| Residential AC filters | ~$3.5B/year |
| HVAC maintenance & repair | ~$12B/year |
| HVAC replacement (new units) | ~$18B/year |
| B2B HVAC data/intelligence | Emerging — no dominant player |

FilterDrop targets a thin slice of all three with a platform play: subscription revenue funds data collection; data revenue funds subscriber growth.

## 3. Solution

FilterDrop operates as a two-sided platform:

**Side 1 — Consumer**: "Set it and forget it" filter delivery. Register your AC unit once, receive the right filter on the right schedule, never think about it again.

**Side 2 — Industry**: Aggregated, anonymized AC unit data delivered as intelligence reports and lead feeds to contractors, manufacturers, and commercial property managers.

### How It Works (Consumer)

1. Customer visits FilterDrop.com and registers their AC unit (brand, model, install year, SEER rating, tonnage, zip code)
2. FilterDrop looks up the manufacturer's recommended filter replacement interval and filter spec
3. Customer selects a subscription plan and payment method
4. Filters ship automatically on schedule — no action required
5. Customer receives optional alerts: "Your unit is 11 years old. The average lifespan for this model is 12–15 years. Would you like a free quote from a local HVAC contractor?"

### How It Works (Industry)

1. Contractors and manufacturers subscribe to regional intelligence reports
2. Reports include: unit age distribution by zip code, brand/model breakdown, filter compliance rates, units approaching end-of-life, common reported issues
3. Contractors receive opt-in leads: households with units 10+ years old in their service area
4. Manufacturers receive anonymized field feedback by model line

## 4. Products & Services

### 4a. Consumer Filter Subscriptions (B2C)

| Tier | Interval | Price/mo | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | 90 days | $15 | Standard MERV 8, single filter |
| Standard | 60 days | $22 | MERV 11, improved allergen capture |
| Premium | 30 days | $38 | MERV 13, best for allergy/asthma households |
| Custom | Variable | Quoted | Multi-unit homes, oversized systems |

All tiers include:
- Auto-sized filter based on registered unit model
- Free shipping
- Email reminders 7 days before delivery
- Unit profile dashboard

### 4b. Commercial Subscriptions (B2B)

Target customers: property management companies, HOAs, small businesses, office buildings, retail locations.

- Volume discounts (5+ units: 15% off, 20+ units: 25% off)
- Centralized billing and delivery management portal
- **Depreciation Schedule Reports**: automatically generated asset schedules for each registered HVAC unit, including:
  - Original install date and estimated useful life
  - Current book value (straight-line depreciation)
  - Recommended replacement year
  - IRS/tax depreciation guidance (MACRS 5-year property)
- **Government Incentive Alerts**: notify commercial customers when their aging units qualify for federal tax credits (IRA Section 25C), local utility rebates, or ENERGY STAR replacement incentives based on SEER improvement

### 4c. Data Intelligence Products

Sold to HVAC contractors, manufacturers, and property data companies.

| Product | Buyer | Price | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regional Age Report | HVAC contractors | $99–$499/mo | Monthly |
| End-of-Life Lead Feed | HVAC dealers/installers | $199–$999/mo | Weekly |
| Manufacturer Field Feedback | OEMs | Custom contract | Quarterly |
| ZIP-Level Unit Census | Insurers / Real estate data | Custom | Annual |

### 4d. Referral Program

When a subscriber's unit reaches the end of its manufacturer-recommended lifespan, FilterDrop:
1. Notifies the homeowner with educational content ("Your Carrier unit is now 13 years old — here's what to expect")
2. Offers a curated quote from 2–3 pre-vetted local HVAC installers
3. Earns a referral commission of $75–$250 per completed installation

This is opt-in for the homeowner and transparent. FilterDrop does not sell personal data — it generates qualified inbound leads for contractors who pay for access.

### Unit Economics (B2C)

- Average subscription: $25/month = $300/year
- Filter COGS + shipping: ~$9/delivery = ~$54/year (at 6 deliveries)
- Gross margin per subscriber: ~82%
- Customer acquisition cost (target): <$35
- Payback period: ~1.5 months
- LTV at 36-month avg retention: ~$900 → LTV:CAC ratio ~25:1

### Unit Economics (Data)

- Marginal cost of adding a data subscriber: ~$0 (data already collected)
- Infrastructure cost scales slowly with volume (Supabase + analytics)
- Data revenue is nearly pure margin above ~$5k/mo operating cost

## 7. Data Strategy

### Data Collected at Registration

| Field | Source | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Brand | User input | Brand-level analytics, manufacturer reports |
| Model number | User input | Filter size lookup, lifespan lookup |
| Install year | User input | Age calculations, replacement timing |
| SEER rating | User input / model lookup | Efficiency benchmarking, incentive matching |
| Tonnage | User input / model lookup | Filter size, energy modeling |
| ZIP code | User input | Geographic segmentation |
| Filter change history | Platform behavior | Compliance analytics |

### Data Governance

- All data sold externally is **anonymized and aggregated** — no individual records
- Minimum aggregation threshold: no report includes data from fewer than 50 units in a given area
- Users opt into data sharing at registration (default: opted in, clearly explained)
- Users can opt out at any time without affecting their subscription
- Privacy policy is plain-language and reviewed annually

### Data Products Roadmap

**Year 1**: Regional age distribution reports (zip code level)

**Year 2**: Predictive failure scoring by brand/model/age/climate zone

**Year 3**: Integration with smart thermostat data (opt-in) for real-time efficiency and anomaly detection

## 9. Operations

### Filter Fulfillment

- **Model**: Pure drop-ship, no owned inventory
- **Suppliers**: Filterbuy wholesale, Nordic Pure, or US Air Filters (negotiate drop-ship agreements)
- **Process**: Subscription trigger → order API → supplier ships directly to customer → tracking passed to customer
- **Target COGS**: <40% of subscription price including shipping

### Customer Support

- **Year 1**: Founder-led via email + Intercom chat
- **Year 2**: 1–2 support hires as volume grows
- Self-service: subscription pause, filter size update, unit profile edit

### Compliance & Legal

- Privacy policy and data sharing terms reviewed by counsel before launch
- CCPA compliance (California residents)
- Data licensing agreements reviewed and countersigned before any data is sold

## 12. Competitive Moat

1. **Data network effect**: Every subscriber adds to the dataset. At scale, the intelligence product becomes impossible for new entrants to replicate quickly.

2. **Switching cost**: Subscriber's unit profile is stored on the platform. Changing services means re-registering all units.

3. **Contractor stickiness**: Once HVAC contractors build their acquisition pipelines around FilterDrop lead feeds, they have a strong incentive to stay as the dataset grows.

4. **First-mover in data layer**: No current filter subscription company has a data monetization strategy. Moving first establishes relationships with manufacturers and contractors before competitors notice.

## 13. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amazon launches a competing data product | Low | High | Build contractor relationships fast; data moat takes years to replicate |
| Data privacy regulation tightens | Medium | Medium | Aggressive opt-in model, anonymization from day 1, legal review |
| Drop-ship supplier reliability issues | Medium | Medium | Qualify 2–3 suppliers, maintain small safety stock for top SKUs |
| Low data opt-in rates | Medium | Medium | Make opt-in default, explain value exchange clearly, offer discount for opting in |
| Slow B2C subscriber growth | Medium | High | Diversify channels early; commercial accounts provide revenue floor |
| Filter margin compression | Low | Low | Data/referral revenue provides margin buffer; can absorb filter price pressure |
